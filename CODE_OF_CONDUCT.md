# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

As members, contributors, and maintainers of this project, we pledge to create an open, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone. We are committed to building a community based on mutual respect, collaboration, and learning.

We commit to:

* Treat all people with respect and consideration.
* Listen to different perspectives and accept constructive feedback.
* Promote a safe environment free from harassment.
* Collaborate positively to improve the project and the community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

* Using welcoming and inclusive language.
* Giving and accepting constructive feedback.
* Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members.
* Respecting different opinions, experiences, and perspectives.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Use of offensive language, insults, or personal attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* Discrimination or exclusion of individuals or groups.
* Posting inappropriate, harmful, or abusive content.

## Consultation

If you have questions about expected behavior or need guidance, you may contact the project maintainers through the official communication channels of the repository.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including but not limited to:

* Code repositories
* Issues
* Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Documentation
* Any communication channels related to the project

## Enforcement Responsibility

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and for taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly while respecting the privacy and security of the reporter.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Depending on the severity of the behavior, actions may include:

* A private warning.
* A request for behavior correction.
* Removal of offensive content.
* Temporary suspension from participation.
* Permanent ban from the community.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by open community standards and best practices used in collaborative open-source software projects.
